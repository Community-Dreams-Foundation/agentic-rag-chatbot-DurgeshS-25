SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

The organization maintains a layered security posture built on three
pillars: preventive controls, detective monitoring, and rapid incident
response. This report summarizes the current state of security operations,
key metrics, compliance status, and strategic priorities for FY2025.

Key Highlights:
- Mean Time to Detect (MTTD): 18 minutes (industry benchmark: 45 minutes)
- Mean Time to Respond (MTTR): 1.4 hours (industry benchmark: 3.2 hours)
- Zero critical vulnerabilities outstanding as of March 2025
- SOC 2 Type II audit completed with clean opinion
- Phishing simulation click rate reduced from 9.1% to 4.3% (YoY)

SECTION 2: SECURITY ARCHITECTURE

2.1 Identity and Access Management
All production systems enforce multi-factor authentication (MFA) using
TOTP or hardware security keys. Privileged access is managed via
just-in-time (JIT) provisioning with a maximum session duration of 4 hours.
Role-based access control (RBAC) is applied across all internal platforms.

2.2 Network Security
- Perimeter: Next-generation firewall with deep packet inspection
- Internal segmentation: Zero-trust network architecture
- Monitoring: 24/7 SOC with SIEM integration (Splunk)
- Threat intelligence feeds updated every 15 minutes

2.3 Endpoint Security
All endpoints run EDR (Endpoint Detection and Response) software.
Patch compliance for critical vulnerabilities is maintained at 99.1%
within a 48-hour SLA. Non-compliant endpoints are automatically
quarantined pending remediation.

2.4 Data Protection
Encryption at rest: AES-256
Encryption in transit: TLS 1.3 (minimum)
Key management: HashiCorp Vault (HSM-backed)
Data Loss Prevention (DLP) policies enforced on all outbound channels.

SECTION 4: INCIDENT RESPONSE

4.1 Severity Classification

P1 - Critical: Full platform outage or confirmed data breach
     Response SLA: 15 minutes | Escalation: CEO, CTO, CISO, Legal
P2 - High: Major functionality degradation affecting >25% of users
     Response SLA: 1 hour | Escalation: CTO, Engineering VP, CISO
P3 - Medium: Minor degradation with available workaround
     Response SLA: 4 hours | Escalation: Engineering Lead
P4 - Low: Cosmetic or minimal impact issue
     Response SLA: 2 business days | Escalation: Assigned team

4.2 FY2024 Incident Summary
Total incidents reported: 47
- P1: 2 (both resolved within SLA; post-mortems completed)
- P2: 8 (7 resolved within SLA; 1 required exception approval)
- P3: 24 (all resolved within SLA)
- P4: 13 (all resolved within SLA)

No data breaches or regulatory notifications were required in FY2024.

4.3 Post-Incident Process
All P1 and P2 incidents require a post-mortem document within 5
business days of resolution. Post-mortems follow a blameless format
covering: timeline, root cause, impact, remediation, and prevention.
